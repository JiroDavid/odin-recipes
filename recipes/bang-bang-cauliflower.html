Bang Bang Cauliflower

Head back to our Home Page

Try a veggie twist on bang bang chicken.

Replace the meat with cauliflower florets. With a crispy coating and a spicy sauce it makes a great starter or side.

Here are the ingredients:

  • 125g plain flour
  • 1 tsp paprike
  • 1 tsp garlic granules
  • 200ml milk
  • 75g panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 small head cauliflower
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 red chilli
  • 4 spring onions (grated)

    • For the sauce:

  • 80g sweet chilli sauce
  • 1 lime
  • 20g sriracha
  • 1 tsp maple syrup

Method:

  1. Heat the oven to220C/200C fan/gas 7. Line a baking tray with baking parchment.
  2. Put the flour, paprika, garlic granules, a large grind of black pepper and a pinch of salt in a large bowl.
  3. Gradually whisk in the milk until you have a smooth batter that's thick enough to coat a spoon.
  4. Put the breadcrumbs in a shallow bowl. Toss each floret in the batter mix, tap off any excess, then toss in the breadcrumbs to coat.
  5. Transfer to the lined baking tray, and repeat with all the cauliflower florets.
  6. Bake for 25-30 mins until golden brown and crispy, turning halfway through so they bake evenly.
  7. Meanwhile, put all the ingredients for the sauce in a bowl and whisk to combine, lightly seasoning with salt and pepper.
  8. Put the cooked cauliflower in a large bowl and mix through the sauce until they're all coated.
  9. Sprinkle over the sesame seeds so they stick to the sauce, and top with the chilli and spring onions.
